Handbag

This one-of-a-kind bag has lots of inside pockets and contains a mixture of new and reclaimed fabrics. The front pocket panel is handwoven from a mix of cotton and cotton-acrylic yarns, some with thick and thin properties which create textural interest.

This prototype is perfect for everyday use. I know this as I have been testing it. I get a lot of admiring comments and look forward to making more in other colours and with different handwoven fabrics.

Water Lilies Luxury Scarf

Some weavers complain about the work involved in putting the long vertical warp threads on the loom. I don't. It's one of my absolute favourite things to do. I think the warp for this scarf was one of the longest to prepare as every warp strand is made up of two threads. I hope you agree that it is reminiscent of Monet's paintings of water lilies.

The combination of cotton and bamboo make for a luxuriously soft feel on the neck.

Butterfly Wall Art

Yes vegan 'taxidermy' is a thing these days - what fun! Imagine animal trophy heads made from knitting or vibrant fabrics. Seeing these inspired me to make some butterflies of my own. I fully indulge my joy of combining colourful yarns to make the fabric for these and then combine with offcuts stitched on with multiple coloured threads. The antennae are made from garden wire wrapped with cotton yarn and the velvet ribbon is reclaimed.

Nursery, hallway, living room - where would you put yours?
