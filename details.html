Handbag

This one-of-a-kind bag has lots of inside pockets and contains a mixture of new and reclaimed fabrics. The front pocket panel is handwoven from a mix of cotton and cotton-acrylic yarns, some with thick and thin properties which create textural interest.

This prototype is perfect for everyday use. I know this as I have been testing it. I get a lot of admiring comments and look forward to making more in other colours and with different handwoven fabrics.

Tech Bag

No-one else will have a bag quite like this. It's made from a mix of riotously joyful handwoven orange mostly cotton fabric and reclaimed teal curtain remnants. It's padded with thick cotton calico to give some protection to your gadgets.

Going to meetings will definitely be much more fun when you can carry your tech in a beautiful bag such as this.

Meadow Dreams Scarf and Bag

All the colours of a summer meadow are here: little wild orchids, in jewel tones, are hiding amongst a wide variety of green grass shades.

There is also a handy zip bag made from a sturdy grey cotton linen and featuring matching handwoven fabric.

Autumn Fire Scarflet

Many of my customers appreciate a short neck warmer, without lots of scarf dangling and getting in their way. This one has all the vibrant shades of deciduous woodland late in the season.

It features a UK-made button and a chinese-style button closure.

Reusable Gift Bag

We are all trying to do our bit for the environment and present wrappings are so often used so briefly. Here's an alternative form of present wrapping which is a gift in itself. The outer is made from recycled curtain fabric and features a handwoven red cotton heart.

Imagine arriving at a party with one of these beauties.

Storage Pocket

For those who like more subtle colours, this storage pocket will compliment any green, blue, or beige decor. It can be folded up and put away when not in use. Surely it's too uniquely lovely to be hidden away though?!

I definitely want to make more of these in different colours as they don't hang around in my shop for long.

Prism Laptop Case

As I love bright colours, of course I adore making rainbow fabrics. This one features organised, symmetrical stripes with a central flash of yellow.

Channel your inner rock god with this David Bowie style case.

Water Lilies Luxury Scarf

Some weavers complain about the work involved in putting the long vertical warp threads on the loom. I don't. It's one of my absolute favourite things to do. I think the warp for this scarf was one of the longest to prepare as every warp strand is made up of two threads. I hope you agree that it is reminiscent of Monet's paintings of water lilies.

The combination of cotton and bamboo make for a luxuriously soft feel on the neck.

Butterfly Wall Art

Yes vegan 'taxidermy' is a thing these days - what fun! Imagine animal trophy heads made from knitting or vibrant fabrics. Seeing these inspired me to make some butterflies of my own. I fully indulge my joy of combining colourful yarns to make the fabric for these and then combine with offcuts stitched on with multiple coloured threads. The antennae are made from garden wire wrapped with cotton yarn and the velvet ribbon is reclaimed.

Nursery, hallway, living room - where would you put yours?
